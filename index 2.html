Methodology

How profiles are built

STEP 01

Disclosed money

Start with AEC disclosure data. Record donor, recipient, amount, date/year and source URL for every reportable donation.

STEP 02

Business interests

Map companies, ownership, industries, investments, government contracts and policy exposure using ASIC, AusTender and public filings.

STEP 03

Policy alignment

Identify policies that plausibly affect those interests: tax, approvals, energy, migration, defence, procurement and industrial relations.

STEP 04

Benefit test

Balance public benefits against public costs. Jobs and tax matter. So do subsidies, environmental costs, housing pressure and influence concentration.

Scoring model

Australia benefit test

Public benefit indicators

  • + Local jobs and wages
  • + Tax and royalties paid
  • + Productive infrastructure
  • + Export earnings
  • + Innovation or capability

Public cost indicators

  • − Subsidies and procurement risk
  • − Environmental externalities
  • − Housing or migration pressure
  • − Market concentration
  • − Political influence imbalance
