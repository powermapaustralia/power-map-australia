About this project

Honest, evidence-based, transparent

The gap in Australian transparency isn't the data — the AEC publishes donations, AusTender publishes contracts, ASIC publishes company records. The gap is comprehension. Power Map connects that data into a readable chain: who has money → who they fund → what policies change → who benefits → who pays.

Every entity gets the same framework regardless of ideology. Mining magnates and union bosses, property developers and banks — the question is always the same: what do they want from government, and what does the public gain or lose?

Evidence tiers

Every claim is tiered by evidence quality:

Verified — AEC register, ASIC, Hansard, court findings, ANAO
Reported — ABC, AFR, Guardian, SMH with article link
Alleged — Advocacy/NGO research, clearly labelled

Legal standard

Donation ≠ corruption. Policy alignment ≠ wrongdoing. This site maps relationships and asks conflict-of-interest questions. Stronger language (misconduct, corruption, wrongdoing) requires court findings or regulator determinations — and is labelled accordingly.

Methodology

1. Disclosed money (AEC register)
2. Business interests (ASIC, annual reports)
3. Policy exposure mapping
4. Outcome analysis (Hansard, ANAO, media)
5. Benefit/cost assessment — public interest framing

What we don't do

We don't publish unverified donation amounts. We don't make criminal allegations without evidence. We don't treat one side of politics differently. We don't remove VERIFY flags until data is confirmed from primary sources.
